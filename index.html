The Plot Twist: Theory vs. Reality

Sometimes the data tells a different story than the textbooks

üìä Box's M Test: A statistical test that checks whether covariance matrices are equal across groups. H‚ÇÄ: Equal covariance (LDA assumption). H‚ÇÅ: Unequal covariance (QDA assumption).

What Theory Said

Box's M Test: p < 0.05

We rejected the null hypothesis of equal covariance. This means the covariance matrices differ significantly between diabetes and no-diabetes groups.

Statistical theory clearly suggests QDA should win (since it allows different covariances).

The textbooks were confident...

What Data Revealed

LDA Won by 4.79%

Despite the violation, LDA was more robust. QDA likely overfitted the noise in the smaller class.

...but reality had other plans

üí° Key Insight: In real-world scenarios with imbalanced data, simpler models often outperform complex ones. Robustness beats theoretical perfection.
